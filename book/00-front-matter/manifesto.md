# The Principal Engineer Handbook Manifesto

Architecture is not measured by elegance, but by longevity. A good design survives change without hiding its costs.

Every API is a promise. It defines what other people and systems are allowed to trust.

Every dependency is a decision. It brings behavior, failure modes, ownership, and upgrade pressure into the system.

Every abstraction is a liability until proven useful. It must reduce real complexity, not rename it.

Documentation is shared memory. Without it, teams repeat debates, lose context, and rediscover old failures.

Simplicity is an engineering investment. It buys reviewability, operability, and room for future change.

Technical debt is not created by compromise, but by undocumented compromise. A known trade-off can be managed; a forgotten one becomes risk.

The best engineers ask better questions. They expose constraints before they optimize solutions.

The goal of a Principal Engineer is to leave the system and team stronger than they found them.
